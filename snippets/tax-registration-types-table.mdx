### European Union (All EU Member States)

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | Traditional VAT registration in a specific EU country | You have physical presence, warehouse, or significant operations in that country. File returns locally. |
| **OSS for EU** | One-Stop Shop for EU-established businesses selling B2C across the EU | You're EU-based and sell to consumers in multiple EU countries. File one quarterly return covering all EU sales. |
| **OSS for Non-EU** | One-Stop Shop for businesses outside the EU selling to EU consumers | You're based outside the EU and sell digital services, goods, or other products to EU consumers. |
| **IOSS** | Import One-Stop Shop for low-value imported goods (≤€150) | You sell or facilitate sales of imported goods valued at €150 or less from outside the EU to EU consumers. |

**EU Member States**: Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden

### United Kingdom

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | UK VAT registration | You're established in the UK or exceed the £90,000 registration threshold for UK sales. |
| **Non-established taxable person (NETP)** | Registration for non-UK businesses selling in the UK | You're based outside the UK but sell to UK customers and need to charge UK VAT. |

### Australia

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | GST registration with ABN | You exceed AUD $75,000 in annual turnover (AUD $150,000 for non-profits). |
| **Simplified GST** | Simplified registration for foreign businesses | You're based outside Australia and sell to Australian consumers. |

### Canada

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **GST/HST** | Federal Goods and Services Tax / Harmonized Sales Tax registration | You exceed CAD $30,000 in taxable supplies in a calendar quarter or over four consecutive calendar quarters. |
| **QST** | Quebec Sales Tax registration (separate from federal) | You make taxable supplies in Quebec. QST is administered separately from GST/HST. |

### United States

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | Sales tax permit in a specific state | You have nexus (physical or economic) in that state and exceed state-specific registration thresholds. |
| **Marketplace Facilitator** | Registration for platforms facilitating sales | You operate a marketplace and are responsible for collecting tax on behalf of sellers in certain states. |

**Note**: The US has no federal sales tax. Each state has its own rules, rates, and registration requirements.

### New Zealand

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | GST registration | You exceed NZD $60,000 in taxable supplies in a 12-month period. |
| **Offshore supplier** | Registration for non-resident businesses | You're based outside New Zealand and sell remote services, low-value goods, or digital products to NZ consumers. |

### Switzerland

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | VAT registration | You exceed CHF 100,000 in annual turnover (goods/services) or import goods worth CHF 10,000+ annually. |

### Norway

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | MVA (moms) registration | You exceed NOK 50,000 in taxable turnover over 12 months. |
| **VOEC** | VAT on E-Commerce registration for foreign businesses | You're based outside Norway and sell to Norwegian consumers. |

### Singapore

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | GST registration | You exceed SGD $1 million in taxable turnover in a 12-month period. |
| **OVR** | Overseas Vendor Registration for digital services | You're an overseas business selling digital services to Singapore consumers and exceed SGD $100,000 in annual global turnover. |

### South Africa

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | VAT registration | You exceed ZAR 1 million in taxable supplies over 12 months. |

### Japan

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | Consumption tax registration | You exceed JPY 10 million in taxable sales in the base period (typically two years prior). |

### South Korea

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | VAT registration | You conduct business activities in South Korea. Most businesses must register regardless of turnover. |

### India

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | GST registration | You exceed the state-specific threshold (typically INR 40 lakhs for goods, INR 20 lakhs for services). |
| **OIDAR** | Registration for Online Information and Database Access or Retrieval services | You're a non-resident supplier providing digital services to Indian consumers. |

### United Arab Emirates

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | VAT registration | You exceed AED 375,000 in taxable supplies and imports over 12 months. |

### Saudi Arabia

| Registration Type | Description | Use Case |
|------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard** | VAT registration | You exceed SAR 375,000 in annual taxable supplies. |

---

<Note>
This table provides general guidance. Tax registration requirements and thresholds change regularly. Always verify current requirements with local tax authorities or a tax professional before registering.
</Note>
